# Inequality: Wealth Concentration and Social Stratification

## Overview

Economic inequality—the unequal distribution of wealth and income—has reached extreme levels in many societies. This inequality affects health, happiness, social cohesion, political stability, and economic efficiency. Understanding its causes, consequences, and potential solutions is crucial for addressing humanity's problems.

## Current Inequality Levels

- Top 1% owns ~40% of wealth
- Top 10% owns ~75% of wealth
- Bottom 50% owns ~2% of wealth
- Billionaires' wealth exceeds GDP of most nations

## Causes of Inequality

### Economic Factors

- **Returns to capital > economic growth**: Piketty's r > g
- **Winner-take-all markets**: Technology, globalization
- **Skill-biased technological change**: Automation
- **Globalization**: Capital mobile, labor not
- **Network effects**: Success breeds success

### Political Factors

- **Policy choices**: Tax cuts, deregulation
- **Political capture**: Wealthy influence policy
- **Declining labor power**: Union decline
- **Rent-seeking**: Extracting value without creating

### Social Factors

- **Assortative mating**: Similar people marry
- **Educational inequality**: Unequal opportunity
- **Inheritance**: Wealth concentration across generations
- **Social capital**: Networks and connections

## Consequences of Inequality

### Health and Well-being

- Lower life expectancy for poor
- Higher stress and mental illness
- Reduced subjective well-being
- Status anxiety across spectrum

### Social Cohesion

- Reduced trust
- Increased crime
- Political polarization
- Social fragmentation

### Economic Efficiency

- Reduced demand
- Underutilized human capital
- Rent-seeking waste
- Financial instability

### Political Stability

- Democratic erosion
- Populist backlash
- Revolution risk
- Authoritarian appeal
