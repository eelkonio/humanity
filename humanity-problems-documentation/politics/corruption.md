# Corruption: Abuse of Power for Private Gain

## Overview

Corruption—the abuse of entrusted power for private gain—is universal across political systems. It undermines governance, economic development, social trust, and human well-being. Understanding corruption's causes and consequences reveals deep problems in human political organization.

## Forms of Corruption

- **Bribery**: Payment for favorable treatment
- **Embezzlement**: Theft of public funds
- **Nepotism**: Favoring relatives
- **Patronage**: Rewarding supporters
- **Regulatory capture**: Industries controlling regulators
- **State capture**: Elites controlling state

## Causes

### Individual Level

- Greed and self-interest
- Opportunity and low risk
- Rationalization and moral disengagement

### Institutional Level

- Weak oversight
- Low accountability
- Poor pay for officials
- Complex regulations
- Monopoly power

### Cultural Level

- Norms accepting corruption
- Low social trust
- Weak rule of law

## Consequences

- Economic inefficiency
- Reduced growth
- Increased inequality
- Eroded trust
- Political instability
- Human rights violations
