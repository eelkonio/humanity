# Sustainability Challenges: Systemic Barriers to Long-term Viability

## Overview

Achieving sustainability—meeting present needs without compromising future generations—requires overcoming coordination problems, short-term thinking, vested interests, and systemic inertia. These challenges reveal fundamental limits of current economic and political systems.

## Barriers to Sustainability

### Economic Barriers

- Growth imperative
- Externalized costs
- Short-term profit focus
- Discount future
- Vested interests

### Political Barriers

- Electoral cycles
- Lobbying power
- International coordination
- Sovereignty concerns
- Regulatory capture

### Social Barriers

- Consumption norms
- Status competition
- Lifestyle lock-in
- Cultural inertia
- Inequality

### Psychological Barriers

- Present bias
- Optimism bias
- Psychological distance
- Complexity
- Helplessness
