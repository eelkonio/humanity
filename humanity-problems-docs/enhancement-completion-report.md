### 2. Final Synthesis Document Created

**[The Core Problems of Humanity: A Synthesis](docs/synthesis-core-human-problems.md)** ✅

This comprehensive synthesis document identifies and explains the five fundamental problems underlying all human dysfunction:

1. **The Cognitive Architecture Problem**: System 1 dominance in System 2 environments
2. **The Social Psychology Problem**: Tribal minds in global societies  
3. **The Temporal Mismatch Problem**: Immediate survival minds in long-term challenge environments
4. **The Complexity Problem**: Simple minds in complex systems
5. **The Coordination Problem**: Individual rationality creating collective irrationality

The synthesis includes:
- Detailed explanations of each core problem
- Cross-level manifestations (individual, group, societal, historical)
- Interconnections and cascade effects between problems
- Implications for human flourishing
- Realistic approaches for working with human nature

## Key Insights from Synthesis

The synthesis reveals several crucial insights about humanity's fundamental problems:

### Universal Patterns
All human problems stem from evolutionary mismatch—sophisticated psychological mechanisms operating in environments different from those that shaped them.

### Interconnected Nature
The five core problems interact and amplify each other through complex feedback loops, creating cascading dysfunction across all levels of human organization.

### Realistic Solutions
Effective approaches must work with rather than against human nature, focusing on environmental and institutional design rather than expecting humans to transcend their evolved psychology.

### Humility and Acceptance
Understanding human constraints provides the foundation for more effective, humane, and sustainable approaches to reducing suffering and promoting flourishing.
